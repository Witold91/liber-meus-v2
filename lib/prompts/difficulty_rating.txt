Guidelines for difficulty:
- "easy": The action is straightforward with little risk. Success is nearly guaranteed. (e.g., picking up an unguarded object, moving to an empty room)
- "medium": The action has meaningful risk or requires some skill/luck. Outcome is uncertain. (e.g., sneaking past a distracted guard, picking a simple lock)
- "hard": The action is risky or highly difficult. Failure is more likely than success. (e.g., overpowering an alert guard, crossing an open area under surveillance)

Guidelines for relevance:
- true: the action plausibly advances the hero's goal, solves a problem, gathers information, or interacts meaningfully with the situation
- false: the action is pointless, self-defeating, or completely unrelated to what the hero is trying to accomplish (e.g., a prisoner trying to escape who decides to eat a flower)

Consider the current state of actors (their alertness, position) and objects (their accessibility) when rating difficulty.
